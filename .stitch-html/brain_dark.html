Synthesis: C. Elegans lifespan

Initial findings from the new assay show a significant increase in lifespan when exposed to compound X under low-stress conditions. We observed a roughly 25% extension in median survival compared to the control group.

Methodology Outline

  • Synchronized L4 larvae were transferred to NGM plates containing 50μM of compound X.
  • Plates were maintained at 20°C.
  • Scoring was performed every 2 days; animals were marked dead if they failed to respond to gentle prodding with a platinum wire.

Preliminary Data & Observations

The animals not only lived longer but also appeared to maintain mobility longer into chronological age. This suggests a potential extension of healthspan as well as lifespan, which is critical for our overall hypothesis.

Note to self: Cross-reference these behavioral findings with the oxidative stress resistance assays completed last month. There might be a correlation with the DAF-16 nuclear localization.

Next steps will involve scaling up the assay and repeating it at 25°C to induce mild thermal stress and see if the protective effect holds.
